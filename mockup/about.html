Abstract of the SGL ERC project

SGL: Beyond the Course. A Knowledge Site for Rethinking Saussure’s General Linguistics
Our goal is to provide an integrated set of critically annotated editions of Ferdinand de Saussure’s (1857-1913) texts on General Linguistics (GL) including: a) the many attempts at writing a book on GL (1891-1902); b) his lectures in GL (1906-1911); c) the Course in General Linguistics (CGL, 1916). The stakes are high: the Saussurean conceptual framework as conveyed by CGL supplied the common ground for European 20th-Century humanities, and Saussure’s vision is still able to bring key contributions to contemporary social sciences (a theory of institutions, or the notion of difference). Earlier Saussurean philology worked to untangle the layers of CGL showing how it came out as an editorial product. Today, in the quest for the genuine Saussure’s GL, autograph writings, lectures and CGL are being opposed to each other, scholars arguing for the primacy of one source over the others. As a result, crucial theoretical nodes happen to appear inconsistent, while the big picture keeps fleeing. Now, the CGL is the last crystallization by others of a moving thought, of which unfinished writings and didactic materials are the best witnesses. Editing these sources means rebooting the understanding of Saussure’s GL. To meet this aim, a full-fledged Knowledge Site (KS) will be implemented, hosting consistent critical editions with English uniform translations and in an augmented form, endowed with manuscript facsimiles and multi-layered annotated transcriptions. The KS will provide the ideal environment to examine these texts in depth, shedding light on their mutual relations by means of advanced computational linguistics methods. The KS, whose durability will be ensured, will be made available through a pioneering open-source scholarly Web platform developed in partnership with the Institute of Computational Linguistics (Pisa). It will serve a general public looking for an authoritative text and scholars interested in accessing Saussure’s records in their fascinating complexity.
cross-panel evaluation
The panel SH5 has been chosen, as at the heart of the project is the immense Cultural Heritage left by Saussure reflection on General Linguistics. It is indeed widely recognized his inspirational and pivotal role not only with regard to linguistics and semiology, but also to the wider range of humanities and social sciences: philosophy, aesthetics, history and sociology of art, cultural anthropology, etc. We are dealing with one of the most representative European achievements in 20th-Century Humanities. SH4 has been selected for two main reasons. First, because undoubtedly theoretical linguistics is one of the main topic of our project. Secondly but not less importantly, because a crucial role is played by the methods, techniques and tools of Computational Linguistics adopted within the project, to support the reconstruction of Saussure’s moving thought through the different textual units. We recommend thus a cross-panel evaluation.
